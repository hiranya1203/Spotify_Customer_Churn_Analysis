# Spotify_Customer_Churn_Analysis
Customer churn analysis for Spotify using Python and Tableau to identify churn drivers and revenue impact.
# Project Title
Spotify Customer Churn & Retention Analysis
# Project Objectives
Identify which customers are more likely to churn based on behavioral patterns.
Analyze factors influencing churn, including device usage, listening enagagement and account tenure.
Evaluate the revenue impact of customer churn
# Tools Used
Mockaroo (Customised Data generation)
Python(Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib)
Tableau
Jupyter Notebook
# Key KPI's
Total Users
Active Users
Churn Rate
Total Revenue
Average Monthly Spend
Revenue at Risk
# Insights
About half of the user have churne, indicating a significant customer retention challenge. 
Users aged 35-44 show highest churn tendency, followed by 45-54 age group. this gives us that middle-aged users be less engaged with the platform or more likely to switch services
Tenure Analysis
1 year period has the highest churn rate, suggesting that users who stay past the initial trial period may still leave if long-term values is not perceived.
Device Type
Tablet users show the highest churn rate, while mobile users also experience significant churn. Since mobile is the dominant listening platform, improving the mobile user experience could significantly impact retention.
# Objectives - Answered
What keeps customers?
Customers who tend to remain active typically show:
regular app usage
actively interacting with platform deature such as playlists and recommendations.
This suggests that personalization and content discovery play a key role in customer retention.
Revenue impact of churn
Approximately 50% of the platform's potential revenue is lost due to churn.
# Recommendations
1. Enhance mobile experience
2. Increase personalization and good music recommendations.
3. Target on middle aged users and tablet users.
4. Improve mid-term retention by providing exclusive content or loyalty rewards or ai-driven music recommendations.
